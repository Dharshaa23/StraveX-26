About the Event

About StraveX '26

StraveX '26 is a solution-driven technical hackathon designed to identify overlooked system-level challenges and transform them into intelligent, scalable solutions.

Open to all students across departments, this presentation-based hackathon encourages interdisciplinary collaboration, structured innovation, and real-world problem solving.

How It Works

Hackathon Format

A structured, presentation-focused event that rewards research, innovation, and technical thinking.

🎯

Solution Presentation-Based

Teams research a real-world problem, develop a structured solution, and present in front of expert judges. Clear communication and analytical thinking are as important as the solution itself.

📊

PPT Submission Mandatory

All teams must submit a structured PowerPoint presentation using the official StraveX '26 template before the hackathon day. The PPT forms the basis of evaluation.

⚙️

Prototype Optional

Working prototypes, demos, or proof-of-concepts are not mandatory but earn extra evaluation weightage. Teams that build and demonstrate solutions will be rewarded.

Guidelines

Rules & Eligibility

  • Open to all year students from any department or discipline.
  • Maximum of 5 members per team. Solo participation is allowed but teamwork is encouraged.
  • Interdisciplinary teams are not just allowed — they are encouraged.
  • All team members must be enrolled students of the institution during the event.
  • Each student may register with only one team. Duplicate registrations will be disqualified.
  • A PPT submission is mandatory. Teams without a submitted PPT will not be evaluated.
  • Use the official StraveX '26 template. Deviating from the structure will result in score deduction.
  • Prototype or demo is optional but earns extra evaluation weightage.
  • PPT must be submitted before the registration deadline: 17 March 2026.
  • Teams must follow the prescribed slide structure as outlined in the official template.
  • Late submissions will not be accepted under any circumstances.

Presentation Quality

All participants are expected to maintain a standard of professional and respectful conduct throughout the event. Any form of plagiarism, dishonesty, or misrepresentation of work will lead to immediate disqualification.

Teams are expected to present their own original ideas. Recycled or previously submitted projects are not eligible. AI tools may be used for research assistance but not for generating the entire solution.

Decisions made by the judges and organizing committee are final and binding. Disputes, if any, should be raised with the faculty coordinator before the final results are announced.

Evaluation

Judging Criteria

Each submission will be evaluated across five equally-weighted dimensions by a panel of expert judges. Teams are encouraged to ensure balanced excellence across all criteria.
